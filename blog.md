When historian Charles Weiner looked over a pile of Richard Feynman’s notebooks, he called them a wonderful ‘record of his day-to-day work’. “No, no!”, Feynman objected strongly. “They aren’t a record of my thinking process. They are my thinking process. I actually did the work on the paper.”. So “I write because I don't know what I think until I read what I say.” ~ Flannery O'Connor. I have questions. I seek answer to them. Or sometimes, I follow a random chain of thoughts to the deepest core of my existence. I document that experience. This happens iteratively in no specific order. If you have questions, or this blog adds value to your life, feel free to drop me an [email](mailto:support@srijitmukherjee) or a note on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/srijit-mukherjee/). Note that I have developed my own micro-blogging platform here, where I micro-blog in each of these bigger blogs before. This aligns with how I actually think.
