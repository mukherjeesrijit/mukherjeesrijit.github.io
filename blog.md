When historian Charles Weiner looked over a pile of Richard Feynman’s notebooks, he called them a wonderful ‘record of his day-to-day work’. “No, no!”, Feynman objected strongly. “They aren’t a record of my thinking process. They are my thinking process. I actually did the work on the paper.”. So “I write because I don't know what I think until I read what I say.” ~ Flannery O'Connor. Feel free to read by choosing among the options below - "Research", "Notes", and "Personal". I have questions. I seek answer to them. I document my understanding. This happens iteratively in no specific order.
