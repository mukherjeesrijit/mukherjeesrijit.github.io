Wang, Jinfeng, et al. "A novel Fourier Adjacency Transformer for advanced EEG emotion recognition." MICCAI, 2025.
Electroencephalography (EEG) emotion recognition is a technique that uses recorded electrical activity from the brain to automatically identify human affective states; since they are directly generated by the central nervous system, they are more objective and authentic for studying emotions compared to behavioral signals. EEG signals are inherently periodic in nature, and recent advancements utilize Fourier Analysis Networks (FAN) (Dong, Yihong, et al. "Fan: Fourier analysis networks." arXiv preprint arXiv:2410.02675 (2024)), which are specifically built for periodic signals by introducing the Fourier Analytic Linear (FAL) Layer to decouple periodic and aperiodic representations. This neural architecture is the first method to leverage novel Fourier-inspired modules to extract periodic features from embedded EEG signals, combining the FAL layer and Multi-Head Self-Attention (MHSA) to create Fourier Adjacent Attention (FAA) heads and replacing standard Self-Attention with the Fourier Adjacent Transformer (FAT) for a generalizable framework. In the FAN layer, the MLP layer is concatenated with sine and cosine activations, but in the FAL layer, the activation function in the MLP component is removed to ensure the aperiodic component remains purely linear. To capture inter-relationships between channels $C_i$ and $C_j$ in EEG data of dimension $(C, T)$, FAA uses the FAL layer to project inputs into periodic $(p)$ and aperiodic $(a)$ components for Queries, Keys, and Values such that $Q = [Q_p \parallel Q_a]$, $K = [K_p \parallel K_a]$, and $V = [V_p \parallel V_a]$. This is followed by scaled dot-product attention applied separately to each part, where FAA adds learnable depth-shared adjacency matrices to attention scores via a gating module before applying SoftMax, ultimately producing the output $[Attention_p V_p \parallel Attention_a V_a]$ to retain decoupled representations through the layers. The Fourier Adjacent Transformer (FAT) simplifies this by replacing MHSA with Multi-Head Fourier Adjacency Attention, using Differential Entropy (DE) features followed by patch and positional embedding to obtain input representations, demonstrating how to build domain-specific AI models from scratch. {{ content }}
